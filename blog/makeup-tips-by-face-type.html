Animal Face Types and Makeup: Tips for Every Type

Knowing your facial archetype is one of the most useful tools in your beauty toolkit. Makeup applied with your archetype in mind can feel effortlessly "right" in a way that generic tutorials often miss.

Why Face Type Matters in Makeup

Most makeup tutorials teach universal techniques: contour here, highlight there, liner like this. But these universal tips don't account for the significant variation in facial geometry. The techniques that look stunning on an angular cat face can actually flatten the soft features of a dog face — and vice versa.

Understanding your archetype allows you to apply makeup techniques that work with your natural features rather than fighting them. The result is a more polished, cohesive look that feels authentic to who you are.

Makeup for Dog Face Types

Foundation and Skin

Dog face types typically have naturally warm, expressive features — the goal with base makeup is to enhance that natural warmth. Glowy, luminous finishes work beautifully; heavy matte foundations can flatten the natural radiance of softer features. Consider a dewy foundation or add a liquid highlighter to your moisturizer for a natural glow.

Eyes: Enhance the Openness

The naturally round, expressive eyes of dog face types are a major asset. Techniques that enhance this openness work best:

  • Lower lash line: A light shimmer or light-colored liner on the lower waterline adds brightness and emphasizes eye size.
  • Mascara: Volumizing mascara on both upper and lower lashes enhances the naturally expressive quality.
  • Eyeliner: Soft, smudged liner rather than sharp lines. The soft liner matches the archetype's natural softness and avoids an incongruous sharp look.
  • Eyeshadow: Warm-toned shadows — peaches, bronzes, warm mauves — complement the archetype's inherent warmth beautifully.

Blush: The Dog Type's Best Friend

Blush is particularly transformative on dog face types. Applied to the apples of the cheeks in a soft, rounded application, it enhances the warm, approachable quality of this archetype. Peachy-pink and warm rosy tones look especially natural. Avoid cool-toned blushes (pure pinks or mauves) which can create a jarring contrast with warm features.

Lips

Dog face types already tend toward fuller, more expressive lips. Nudes and warm pinks that complement the natural lip color are always flattering. For a more defined look, slightly overline the cupid's bow. Bold reds in warm tones (coral-reds, brick reds) are stunning on this archetype.

Contouring

Light contouring that adds subtle definition without sharpening is ideal. Focus on the temples and very lightly at the sides of the forehead rather than harsh jaw contouring. The goal is depth, not dramatic reshaping.

Dog Face Makeup Mantra: Enhance warmth, embrace softness, add glow. Techniques that create sharp lines or aggressive definition work against the natural charm of this archetype.

Makeup for Cat Face Types

Foundation and Skin

Cat face types have naturally dramatic features that translate well to both matte and semi-matte base makeup. A more polished, skin-smoothing finish can enhance the sophisticated quality of this archetype. The strong bone structure means the face doesn't need luminosity to create dimension — the features themselves do that work.

Eyes: The Liner Game

Eye makeup is where cat face types can truly shine:

  • Cat eye liner: A winged liner that follows and slightly amplifies the natural upward slant of the eye is an iconic look perfectly suited to this archetype.
  • Smoky eye: The dramatic, angular features of cat face types carry bold, smoky eye looks with authority. This is where this archetype most clearly outperforms other types in makeup versatility.
  • Graphic liner: Bold, geometric liner designs — double wings, floating liner — look particularly striking against the sharp facial geometry.

Blush and Highlight: The Cheekbone Strategy

The high cheekbones of cat face types create a natural canvas for structured blush application. Apply blush slightly higher on the cheekbones and blend upward toward the temples — this technique follows and enhances the natural bone structure. A highlighter swept precisely along the top of the cheekbones creates a sculpted, editorial look.

Lips

Cat face types suit a wide range of lip looks. Bold statement lips — deep reds, berries, plums, even dark nudes — are particularly powerful. The strong facial features provide a "frame" that can support intense lip colors without the overall look becoming unbalanced.

Contouring

Cat face types can benefit from contouring at the temples to balance wide cheekbones. A light contour just below the cheekbones can add dimension. Unlike dog types, moderate contouring on the jaw can add further definition to the natural angles.

Cat Face Makeup Mantra: Amplify the drama, embrace definition. Bold choices suit this archetype's natural strength. Precise, sharp application echoes the face's natural geometry.

Universal Tips for Both Types

  • Well-groomed, defined brows are universally flattering — adjust the tail shape to either slightly soften (dog type) or sharpen (cat type) depending on your goal.
  • Skincare is the foundation of great makeup. Both archetypes benefit from a consistent skincare routine that maintains skin texture and hydration.
  • The best makeup looks are the ones you feel most yourself in — these are guidelines, not rules.
